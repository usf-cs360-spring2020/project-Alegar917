Question

How do evictions caused by Tenant's affordability issues such as Non Payment or Late Payments compare to the other eviction types? Also, what specific neighborhoods are being affected by most of these different types of eviction notice compared to each other?

Answer

To my surprise, evictions notices caused by affordability issues were not the highest it was top 5 at best. The highest reason for eviction was because of a breach of contract or nuisance which was pretty surprising to see. I also was able to categories each type of eviction notice per neighborhood, for example, I will be able to compare what neighborhoods had the most ratio of Non-payment compared to a neighborhood that a breach the highest or compare each neighborhood inside a certain hierarchy. It was interesting to see that Lakeshore which is a neighborhood near the park, golf courses, recreation center, and San Francisco State University was the neighborhood of the most eviction request caused by a breach of contract but also that the ratio of notices compared to second highest which was Mission, was significantly higher.

Encoding

My color encoding is based on the number of eviction notice types, for instance, dark blue represented access_denial or light blue represented breach. For now, I chose a simple color scheme but I will change it later since some types could accidentally be grouped because of a similar color. For visual encoding, the size of the blocks is based on the sum of reports of each category it's under.

Future Interactivity

I plan to add a tooltip for the information for each block like the count for each eviction type and the name for the neighborhood to better see it. I will also like to do a data filtering so I can filter the data by months so you can see the change of the data monthly of the course of the year, and I will add brushing to better see the hierarchy of the data.
